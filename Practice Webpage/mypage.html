A country is a distinct part of the world, such as a state, nation, or other political entity. Because different organizations have different stances on exactly what it takes to qualify as a country, there is no definite answer to how many countries there are in the world. However, the United Nations recognises 193 member states and 2 "observer" states (Vatican City and Palestine). These countries are divided into 6 main continents: Europe, Asia, Africa, Oceania, North America and South America. There are roughly 7,000 languages around the world, but due to modernisation a third of them are risking extinction.
