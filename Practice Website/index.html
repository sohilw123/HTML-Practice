What are ethics?

In order for us to first understand the different schools of ethics, we must first understand ethics itself. Ethics is defined as "the critical, structured examination of how we ought to behave when our behaviour affects others" (Vaughn & MacDonald, 2019). Ethical claims often involve coming to a conclusion about whether a specific action is right or wrong. They are used to determine right from wrong, right and wrong, however, are subjective. So different ways of thinking lead to different conclusions on whether a specific course of action is the right course to take. This has led to different schools of thought in the philosophy of ethics.
